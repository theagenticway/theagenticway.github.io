Multi-Generational Family Trip Plan

Big Island Oahu Maui

The Big Island (Top Pick)

Offers the most space for a large group. The sightseeing (Volcanoes, Waterfalls) is very accessible for grandparents (drive-up views), while the active boys have endless space.

Where to Stay

Four Seasons Hualalai ↗

The ultimate family luxury resort. Features the "King's Pond" (swimmable aquarium) and low-rise bungalows perfect for seniors.

Mauna Lani (Auberge) ↗

Modern luxury with a protected beach cove. Home to the island's best restaurant (CanoeHouse) and cultural activities.

7-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival & Lava Pools

Settle in. Boys snorkel in the resort lagoon; Grandparents relax on the lanai.

Oahu (Dining Winner)

Highest density of restaurants offering concurrent high-end vegan and non-vegan menus. Accessible history at Pearl Harbor.

Where to Stay

Four Seasons Ko Olina ↗

Located on a calm lagoon (safe for kids). Luxury quiet, but walkable to Disney Aulani for character breakfasts.

The Kahala Resort ↗

Secluded luxury near Waikiki. Famous for its private dolphin lagoon which the kids will love.

Maui (Kid's Choice)

Unbeatable pools for the boys and whale watching. Dining requires careful reservations at specific venues.

Where to Stay

Grand Wailea ↗

Legendary pool system: water elevator, rapids, ropes. High energy.

Four Seasons Maui ↗

Located next door. Higher service level, calmer adult pools, spacious rooms.
