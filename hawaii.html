Big Island Oahu Maui

The Big Island

Best for unifying your group. The spaciousness prevents crowding, and the "farm-to-table" ethos of the island naturally supports high-quality meat and vegetable dishes equally.

Where to Stay

Four Seasons Hualalai ↗

The ultimate multi-gen resort. Low-rise bungalows are easy for Adults, and the "King's Pond" (carved lava aquarium) provides safe snorkeling for the boys on-site.

Mauna Lani (Auberge) ↗

Modern, open-air luxury with a protected cove beach. Home to the CanoeHouse restaurant and close to the shops for easy family strolling.

7-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival & Lava Pools

Settle in. Adults can relax on the lanai while the boys snorkel in the resort's protected lagoon.

'Ulu Ocean Grill (On-site)
Standard Menu

75% of the menu is locally sourced fish (Ono, Mahimahi) or charcoal grilled steaks.

Vegetarian

Dedicated Vegan menu available (e.g., Roasted Hearts of Palm, Tofu Curry) prepared in a separate wok.

Day 2: Volcano National Park

Visit Hawaii Volcanoes National Park. Adults can see the crater from accessible paved lookouts; older boys can hike the Kilauea Iki trail across the lava lake.

Kilauea Lodge
Standard Menu

A historic lodge serving hearty game meats like Antelope, Duck, and Catch of the Day.

Vegetarian

Known for their Vegetable Napoleon and Portobello Wellington (request vegan prep).

Day 3: Mantas & Beach

Morning at Hapuna Beach (largest white sand beach). Evening Manta Ray Night Snorkel—a bucket list item for the teens/boys.

Merriman’s Waimea
Standard Menu

The pioneer of Hawaii Regional Cuisine. Mac Nut Crusted Fish and Prime NY Steak.

Vegetarian

Famous for "Dirt Grown" Vegetables, Taro Hummus, and House-made Falafel entrees.

Day 4: Waimea Cowboys

Visit Parker Ranch country. Horseback riding for the kids; historic home tours for the Adults.

CanoeHouse Highlight
Standard Menu

Open-air oceanfront dining featuring Grilled Kona Lobster and Wagyu Beef.

Vegetarian

Offers a separate "MY" (Plant-Based) Menu with grilled specialty veg (Corn Ribs, Mushrooms).

Day 5: Waterfalls

Drive the Hamakua Coast. Akaka Falls has a paved loop (some stairs), while Rainbow Falls offers a view directly from the parking lot.

Lava Lava Beach Club
Standard Menu

Casual "Toes in the sand" dining with Ribs, Burgers, and Fish Tacos.

Vegetarian

Great veggie burgers (check bun ingredients) and large tropical salads.

Day 6: Culture & Coffee

Morning tour at Greenwell Farms (accessible coffee tour). Afternoon: Ladies at Hualalai Spa; Kids at the pool.

Brown's Beach House
Standard Menu

Famous for their Crusted Mahi Mahi and sunset views.

Vegetarian

Chef can prepare a custom 3-course vegan menu if requested 24 hours in advance.

Day 7: Departure

Final swim in the lagoons, brunch at the resort, and transfer to Kona Airport (KOA).

Oahu

The best choice if food is the priority. Oahu offers the highest density of restaurants that can serve a high-end steak dinner and a high-end vegan tasting menu simultaneously.

Where to Stay

Four Seasons Ko Olina ↗

Located on a tranquil, man-made lagoon perfect for kids and seniors. Adjacent to Disney Aulani if you want a character breakfast, but far enough to be quiet.

The Kahala Resort ↗

A legendary luxury hotel secluded on the other side of Diamond Head. Famous for its private dolphin lagoon which provides daily entertainment for children.

7-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival

Check-in and enjoy the lagoon sunset. Easy walking along the Ko Olina path.

Mina’s Fish House
Standard Menu

Michael Mina's Ginger Scallion Whole Fish (spectacular presentation) and seafood towers.

Vegetarian

The kitchen excels at Vegetable Curries and custom Veggie Cakes upon request.

Day 2: Pearl Harbor

Visit the USS Arizona Memorial. It is fully accessible. The 13-year-old and boys can add on the Submarine tour nearby.

Nature Waikiki Top Pick
Standard Menu

Serves a "Sustainable Island French" tasting menu with local meat and seafood.

Vegetarian

Serves a parallel Plant-Based Tasting Menu (same number of courses/timing) so you eat together seamlessly.

Day 3: Jurassic Valley

Head to Kualoa Ranch. Active group does the UTV Raptor tour (muddy fun); Adults take the Movie Sites Bus Tour.

Noe (Italian)
Standard Menu

High-end Italian featuring Truffle Tagliatelle with Veal and Whipped Ricotta.

Vegetarian

Chef is happy to prepare egg-free pasta with Marinara and garden vegetables.

Day 4: North Shore

Watch surfers at Pipeline. Walk through Waimea Valley, a paved botanical garden walk leading to a waterfall (swimmable).

Tane Vegan Izakaya
Standard Menu

Note: This is a fully Vegan sushi spot, but rated world-class by all eaters. Meat eaters usually love it.

Vegetarian

The best plant-based sushi in the Pacific. Intricate rolls, nigiri, and ramen.

Day 5: Polynesian Culture

Spend the afternoon at the Polynesian Cultural Center. Great for kids (canoe pageant, fire making) and very accessible.

Pounders (at PCC Entrance)
Standard Menu

Guava Smoked Ribs and fresh local seafood pizzas.

Vegetarian

Farm fresh salads and veggie pizzas (much higher quality than the luau buffet inside).

Day 6: Diamond Head & City

Active group hikes Diamond Head. Adults can take a trolley tour of Waikiki.

Morimoto Asia
Standard Menu

Peking Duck and A5 Wagyu Beef.

Vegetarian

Ishi Yaki Bop (Rice cooked in hot stone bowl at table—ask for Tofu version) and Yasai Ramen.

Day 7: Departure

Final breakfast at the resort and transfer to Honolulu Airport (HNL).

Maui

If the priority is "Pool Time" for the 4 boys, Maui wins. However, strict vegetarian dining requires more careful planning here than on Oahu.

Where to Stay

Grand Wailea ↗

The "Disney World" of Hawaii pools. Waterslides, a water elevator, rapids, and ropes. It is high energy and busy, which the boys will love.

Four Seasons Maui ↗

Located right next door. Higher service level, calmer adult pools, and spacious rooms. You can walk to Grand Wailea if needed.

7-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival & Pools

Check into the resort and hit the pool immediately. The kids will likely spend the entire afternoon in the Wailea Canyon Activity Pool.

Humuhumunukunukuapua'a
Standard Menu

Polynesian thatched-roof dining serving Fresh Lobster and Steaks over the water.

Vegetarian

Offers Tofu dishes and vegetable curries (Be vigilant about fish sauce here).

Day 2: Whale Watching

December is peak season. Book a catamaran with Pacific Whale Foundation from Ma’alaea Harbor.

Star Noodle
Standard Menu

Famous for their Seared Ahi, Pork Belly, and Singapore Noodles.

Vegetarian

Wok-fried Brussels Sprouts, Hooded Tofu, and Garlic Noodles (ask for egg-free).

Day 3: Upcountry Farm Lunch

Drive up to Kula (cooler air). Visit O’o Farm for a walking tour and gourmet lunch.

O’o Farm Lunch Tour
Standard Menu

The chef grills fresh Catch of the Day or Chicken served with salads you helped pick.

Vegetarian

The chef grills Tofu & Root Veggies separately for a communal, farm-fresh meal.

Day 4: The Luau

Most luaus are buffets (cross-contamination risk). We recommend Feast at Lele which is a sit-down dinner.

Feast at Lele
Standard Menu

5-course meal featuring Kalua Pork, Duck Salad, and Grilled Fish.

Vegetarian

Offers a dedicated Vegan Menu served course-by-course (e.g., Tofu & Broccoli, Grilled Veg).

Day 5: Road to Hana (Lite)

Drive only to Twin Falls (short hike to waterfall) and Ho’okipa Lookout to see windsurfers. Avoid the full drive with young kids.

Paia Fish Market
Standard Menu

The best casual fish sandwiches and plates on the island.

Vegetarian

Cajun Rice and Slaw. *Alternative:* Maka by Mana (Strict Vegan) is just down the street.

Day 6: Resort & Spa

Ladies enjoy the Grand Wailea's Hydrotherapy Circuit. Boys enjoy a final full day at the pools.

Duo Steak and Seafood
Standard Menu

Dry-aged steaks and local seafood in an open kitchen setting.

Vegetarian

Features a separate "Wellness Menu" with creative plant-based options.

Day 7: Departure

Breakfast at the resort and transfer to Kahului Airport (OGG).
